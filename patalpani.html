About Patalpani

Patalpani Waterfall, located around 35 km from Indore, is a mesmerizing natural wonder surrounded by lush greenery. The waterfall is about 300 feet high and is a popular picnic and trekking destination. The name "Patalpani" is derived from a local belief that the water goes deep into the earth, reaching the underworld (Patal in Hindi).

The monsoon season (July to September) is the best time to visit, as the waterfall is in its full glory. However, caution is advised as the water flow can be unpredictable.

Visitors can enjoy the scenic beauty, take photographs, and relax in nature. For adventure seekers, there are trekking trails leading to nearby hills, offering stunning panoramic views of the surroundings.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: Monsoon (July-September) for the best views.
  • Carry enough food and water, as there are limited options nearby.
  • Avoid getting too close to the edge, as the rocks can be slippery.
  • Wear comfortable trekking shoes for a better hiking experience.
  • Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds.
  • Be cautious of the railway track passing nearby, as it is still operational.
  • Consider carrying insect repellent and a raincoat if visiting during the rainy season.
