About Rajwada Palace

Rajwada Palace is an iconic historical landmark in Indore, built by the Holkar dynasty over two centuries ago. This grand seven-story structure showcases an exquisite mix of Maratha, Mughal, and French styles, reflecting the city's rich cultural heritage.

The palace has witnessed many historical events and has been restored multiple times. Today, it stands as a symbol of Indoreâ€™s glorious past, attracting history enthusiasts, architects, and tourists from all over.

Visitors can explore the beautiful gardens, the grand entrance with wooden doors, and the palace interiors that house a museum displaying artifacts, paintings, and sculptures from the Holkar era.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather.
  • Entry Timings: 10 AM - 5 PM; Closed on Mondays.
  • Explore the museum inside to learn about Holkar history.
  • Visit in the evening to witness the beautiful light show.
  • Photography is allowed, but some areas may have restrictions.
  • Nearby attractions include Sarafa Bazaar and Kanch Mandir.
