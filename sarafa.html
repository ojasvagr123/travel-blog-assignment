About Sarafa Bazaar

Sarafa Bazaar is one of Indore’s most iconic destinations, known for its vibrant street food scene that comes alive after sunset. By day, it's a bustling jewelry market, but by night, it transforms into a food lover’s paradise. The market is famous for its delicious variety of snacks, sweets, and local delicacies.

Some must-try dishes include Bhutte Ka Kees, Garadu, Poha Jalebi, Sabudana Khichdi, and the famous Joshi Dahi Bada, where yogurt is thrown into the air before serving!

With a lively atmosphere, mouthwatering flavors, and a mix of tourists and locals, Sarafa Bazaar is a must-visit for anyone exploring Indore.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: After 8 PM when the market is fully active.
  • Try a variety of dishes but start with light snacks before heavy ones.
  • Carry cash, as some vendors might not accept digital payments.
  • Be prepared for crowds, especially on weekends.
  • Stay hydrated, as the spicy food can be intense.
  • Visit in groups to share and try more food items.
  • End your food tour with a glass of cold Shikanji or Kulfi.
